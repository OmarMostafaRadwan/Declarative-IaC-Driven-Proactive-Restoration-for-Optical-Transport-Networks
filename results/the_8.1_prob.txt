"A. Robustness vs. Specialization AnalysisInitial training was conducted on a single degradation model (Ornstein-Uhlenbeck), achieving a prediction Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of 8.10 seconds. However, this performance relies on the assumption that all network faults follow linear drift patterns.To eliminate the 'simulator fallacy' and ensure generalization to unseen failure modes, we retrained the model on a diverse corpus including Exponential, Weibull, and Oscillatory degradation. Under this rigorous multi-physics validation, the model achieves a global MAE of 18.77 seconds. While numerically higher, this metric represents true operational robustness rather than theoretical overfitting.Critically, even under these harsher conditions, the Random Forest model outperforms the Simple Threshold baseline (22.55s) by 16.8% and Linear Extrapolation (88.41s) by 78.8%, with statistical significance verified via paired t-test ($p < 0.001$)."
